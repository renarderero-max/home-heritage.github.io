Mindanao Culture

Mindanao is the southern region of the Philippines known for its diverse cultures, indigenous groups, and rich traditions. Its culture blends Muslim, Christian, and Lumad influences reflected in music, festivals, crafts, and cuisine.

Traditional Homes

Badjao Houses

Stilt houses built over water by the Badjao people of Mindanao's coastal regions.

Tubungan Houses

Elevated wooden homes typical of indigenous communities in Mindanao.

Maranao Houses

Ornate houses with traditional okir carvings from the Maranao people.

Cultural Traditions

Kadayawan Festival

Davao City's festival celebrating bountiful harvests with street dances, floral floats, and cultural shows.

Gong Festival

A celebration of traditional Lumad music, dance, and rituals featuring gongs and indigenous costumes.

Weaving & Crafts

Traditional weaving techniques by Mindanao ethnic groups producing vibrant fabrics and handicrafts.

Churches

San Pedro Church

Historic church in Davao with Spanish-inspired architecture.

Zamboanga Church

Colonial-era church showcasing Mindanaoan-European design.

Cagayan Church

Notable church representing Mindanao’s Christian heritage.

Historical Sites

Agusan Heritage Site

Important landmark showcasing indigenous Mindanaoan culture and history.

Sultan Kudarat Site

Historical site honoring the legacy of Sultan Kudarat and Mindanaoan history.
