Luzon Culture

Luzon is the largest island in the Philippines and a melting pot of diverse cultures, traditions, and ethnic groups. Its culture blends indigenous, Spanish, and American influences visible in festivals, traditional clothing, music, and cuisine.

Bahay Kubo

The Bahay Kubo is a traditional Filipino stilt house made of bamboo and nipa palm. Designed for tropical weather, it showcases rural Filipino life and community culture.

Ifugao Houses

Elevated wooden houses built by the Ifugao people, designed to live harmoniously with the rice terraces and mountainous terrain.

Spanish Colonial Houses

Stone and wood houses from the Spanish colonial era, featuring capiz windows, clay tile roofs, and intricate woodwork.

Panagbenga Festival

Flower festival in Baguio celebrated every February, showcasing floats, street dances, and local artistry.

Pahiyas Festival

Harvest festival in Lucban, Quezon, known for colorful decorations made from rice, fruits, and vegetables to honor San Isidro Labrador.

Weaving & Crafts

Traditional weaving techniques practiced by Luzon ethnic groups, creating colorful fabrics and intricate handicrafts.

San Agustin Church

One of the oldest churches in the Philippines, built during the Spanish era with Baroque architecture.

Paoay Church

UNESCO World Heritage Baroque church famous for its massive buttresses and intricate façade.

Miagao Church

Baroque church with detailed Filipino-European carvings, representing local culture and religious history.

Banaue Rice Terraces

Thousands of years old terraces carved by the Ifugao people, reflecting ingenuity in sustainable agriculture.

Fort Santiago

Spanish colonial fortress in Intramuros, Manila, historically used for defense and imprisonment during colonial times.
